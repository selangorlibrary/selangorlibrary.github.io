History of the Selangor Public Library

The Selangor Public Library Corporation (PPAS) was established through the Selangor Public Library Corporation Enactment 1986 and gazetted in Government Gazette No. 5, dated March 27, 1969 and Government Gazette No.7, 1975 (amendment).

In 1971, a PPAS Board was formed to start a public library in the State of Selangor. The first step in this direction was the acquisition of the Kuala Lumpur Book Club on October 20, 1971 to become a Central Library.

The library was then moved to Shah Alam on November 16, 1986. The design of the library building is a reflection of Bugis culture and its interior design is a clash between Eastern and Western culture. It is also the largest public library in Southeast Asia. On March 15, 1988, the library was opened by the late Sultan Salahuddin Abdul Aziz Shah Alhaj and the building was named Raja Tun Uda and opened to the public.



The 203,600-square-foot (18,922 square meters) building is 6 stories high and can accommodate up to 2,500 people and 400,000 copies of the book at one time. The Opening Ceremony of the New Building was officiated by the Sultan of Selangor, His Highness Sultan Sharafuddin Idris Shah Alhaj Ibni The late Sultan Salahuddin Abdul Aziz Shah Alhaj on Saturday, December 10, 2011 with 14 Muharam 1433H.

The library has some new services to get people interested in coming here. Among the services provided are XD Theater services, 3D Theater, Virtual Reality (VR), Life Sketch Book (LSB), Lego Games and more. The Smart Mobile Library (SML) service is also being introduced to enable users who cannot come here to enjoy the services provided. These services have also been improved to ensure that the library services are in line with current information technology developments. Wifi is provided free of charge and multimedia services are now available on every level. Other online services are also available, including Overdriver, Audiobook Archives and online registration.
